Who We Are

Two Wings. One Mission.

Sanchi Foundation and Sanchaya are sister organisations preserving Karnataka's cultural and literary heritage through technology — freely and forever.

est. 2014

Sanchi Foundation

India's grassroots cultural archiver, working since 2014. Sanchi documents live performances — Carnatic, Hindustani, Yakshagana, Theatre — and makes them freely accessible. Its mission: ensure every performance staged, every raga sung, every lecture delivered leaves a permanent digital trace.

Performance Documentation Video Archive Ninasam Partner Cultural Preservation
est. 2010

Sanchaya (ಸಂಚಯ)

Kannada's digital infrastructure builders, working since 2010. Sanchaya digitises literature, builds language-technology tools, revives historical typefaces, and creates open-access portals — ensuring Kannada thrives in the digital age for students, researchers, and the diaspora alike.

Our Guiding Principles

Built on Openness

Everything we create — recordings, archives, fonts, portals — is freely available to anyone, forever. We believe knowledge belongs to people, not institutions. These principles are not aspirational; they are structural.

View CC BY 4.0 Licence →
⚖️
Open Licensing All content is published under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 — share it, adapt it, build on it, as long as you credit the source.
💻
Open Source Our tools, fonts, and code are published on GitHub under open licences — available for anyone to use, fork, and improve.
📖
Open Knowledge Every archive is freely searchable — no paywalls, no logins, no institutional subscriptions required. Ever.
🔓
Open Access Every portal and every recording is available to anyone with an internet connection — scholar, student, or curious reader.
🎓
Open Education Our resources are usable in classrooms, research papers, and cultural programmes — in Karnataka, across India, and worldwide.
Get Involved

How You Can Contribute

Sanchi Foundation and Sanchaya are built and sustained by a community of people who believe that culture and language deserve a permanent digital home. There are many ways to be part of this work.

🤝

Collaborators

Organisations, institutions, and collectives who share content, expertise, or infrastructure — whose work reaches our shared audience more powerfully together.

You could bring

  • Access to performance recordings or archival material
  • Institutional collections needing a digital home
  • Joint documentation projects or residencies
  • Co-produced research or publications
Collaborate with us →
🌐

Partners

Foundations, CSR programmes, or aligned organisations who support our mission with sustained engagement — formal or informal, project-based or long-term.

Partnership could look like

  • Funding a specific archive or portal project
  • Co-branding a documentation initiative
  • Technology or infrastructure support
  • Visibility & network amplification
Become a partner →
🏛️

Patrons

Individuals who believe in this mission and choose to sustain it with recurring generosity — named in our archives, acknowledged in our work, part of our story.

As a patron you

  • Fund ongoing documentation and digitisation work
  • Are acknowledged in project credits
  • Receive updates on projects your support enables
  • Become part of the permanent archive record
Become a patron →
🙌

Volunteers

People who give time, skill, and energy — from scanning and tagging to writing, translating, designing, or coding open-source tools for Kannada.

Skills we welcome

  • Proofreading and transcription in Kannada
  • Web development and open-source contributions
  • Photography, video, and production support
  • Research assistance for literary archives
Volunteer with us →
❤️

Supporters

Anyone who spreads the word, shares our work, cites our archives, or simply keeps coming back to watch, read, and learn. Every visit, every share, matters.

Ways to support

  • Share videos and portals with your community
  • Cite Sanchaya in academic or journalistic work
  • Follow us on social media and amplify our reach
  • Suggest content or collections we should archive
Follow & share →
